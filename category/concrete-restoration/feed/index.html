Deterioration on your garage floor concrete can occur for any number of reasons.

Here are a few contributing factors:

  • Temperature Changes- concrete expands in the heat and contracts in the cold.
  • Foundation – If the garage floor foundation and concrete surface were poorly installed, to begin with.
  • Soil Displacement – What’s beneath your garage foundation can cause the floor to sink. 
  • Proper Water Drainage – Without it, water damage will stress the surface.
  • High-traffic Area- General wear and tear from spills, leaks, and foot and vehicle traffic.
  • Father Time- Floors will commonly just settle over time.
  • Ohio Winters – Pitting caused by road salt used during the winter months. 

Questions to ask yourself.  How much spalling, pitting, and chipping is there? Is there a structural break? How much material is missing?
